The US National Institute of Health (NIH) has funded many research and have always been pushing and investing into the idea of wearable biosensors. Point-of-care (POC) testing and diagnostics, which offer real-time, remote health monitoring, are a key development area for biosensors. Biosensors make it easier, more accurate, and more sensitive to detect illnesses or infections such as diabetes, cardiovascular disease, cancer, and infectious illness like Coronavirus for example. Microfabricated POC devices will be capable of detecting a wide variety of diseases. POC devices must attain sensitivity similar to lab instruments in order to be utilised as a comprehensive diagnostic tool rather than merely for initial screening of bioagents or diseases. Paper-based biosensors, which reduce the cost and enhance the disposability of biosensors for POC, will see increased demand in poor or developing areas.
